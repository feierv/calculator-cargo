# Client Observations – Air Freight Bidding

Summary of the supplier’s position and fixed local charges for air freight bids. Use for internal reference and when preparing or comparing offers.

---

## Main recommendation

- **Separate air freight and local cost in the bid.**  
  Offer air freight and local charges as distinct lines so the client sees what is fixed (local) vs what can change (air freight).

---

## Observations

### 1. Local vs air freight

- **Local part:** AWB, docs, customs, and trucking are shown separately. Truck cost is a **separate line** on the sheet.
- **Air freight part:** Rates **change frequently** and are often **valid only about 1 week**. Not suitable as a long-term fixed rate.

### 2. POL (Port of Loading)

- Cargo may have **different possible POLs**.  
- **PVG (Shanghai)** is used only as a **reference** in the sheet; other origins may apply.

### 3. Hong Kong / Macau / Taiwan

- Cargo **cannot be transferred** from Hong Kong, Macau, or Taiwan to Chinese mainland for shipping.  
- These lanes are marked **N/A** where not applicable.

### 4. Quotation approach

- Supplier recommends **case-by-case** offers so that quotes stay **timely, accurate, and competitive** rather than using a single long-validity rate.

## Action / follow-up

- Use **case-by-case** pricing for air freight.
- Keep **air freight** and **local (AWB/docs/customs/truck)** clearly separated in bids.
- Treat **PVG** as reference only; confirm POL per shipment.
- Remember **HK/Macau/Taiwan → mainland** transfer is not allowed for shipping.
